i know you well sir and you know me your name i think is adrian i am a roman and my services are as you are against 'em know you me yet the same sir there hath been in rome strange insurrections the people against the senators patricians and nobles the main blaze of it is past but a small thing would make it flame again for the nobles receive so to heart the banishment of that worthy coriolanus that they are in a ripe aptness to take all power from the people and to pluck from them their tribunes forever this lies glowing i can tell you and is almost mature for the violent breaking out banished sir the day serves well for them now i have heard it said the fittest time to corrupt a man's wife is when she's fall'n out with her husband your noble tullus aufidius will appear well in these wars his great opposer coriolanus being now in no request of his country i shall between this and supper tell you most strange things from rome all tending to the good of their adversaries have you an army ready say you i am joyful to hear of their readiness and am the man i think that shall set them in present action so sir heartily well met and most glad of your company well let us go together
